Here's the reframe

Readiness isn't required. It's what the class builds.

Your child doesn't need to sit still, take turns, or follow directions yet. Those are the exact skills a first class starts to grow — gently, through play.

Listening

Tuning in to an instructor's voice in a room full of other kids.

Taking turns

Waiting for a turn, then cheering on the friend who goes next.

Following directions

"Hands up, feet together" — following two-step directions, one game at a time.

Good questions

Preschool parents usually ask…

Is my child too young?

Almost certainly not. The class is built for ages 3–5, and readiness is what it builds — not something your child needs to bring. If they can walk and follow a simple game, they can start.

What if they cry or have a meltdown?

Completely normal, and completely okay. You're right there in the room. If they need to sit with you and watch, that's a fine first class — and you're welcome back next week, free, to try again.

Do parents stay?

Yes. For this age, parents stay in the room and watch the whole time. No drop-off, no separation — you're part of the first class.

What should they wear?

Comfy clothes they can move in — a t-shirt and stretchy pants are perfect. Bare feet or grippy socks. Nothing to buy for the free class.
